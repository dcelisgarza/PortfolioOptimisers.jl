Top recommendation — close the XatRisk Range sign-convention drift

Card 1 is the one where drift is already underway: four hand-inlined Range bodies compute the gain tail under the opposite sign convention from the four delegating one-liners, so the same quantity has two encodings and any tail fix must be proven twice. Converting the two inlined measures to the delegation shape GenericValueatRiskRange already embodies collapses them to one-liners and leaves one convention. It also front-loads the solver-free per-measure test harness that card 2 — the largest pure collapse, 16 drawdown functors → 8 one-liner pairs — needs, so the two make one sweep. Card 4's missing guard is a one-line fix worth taking regardless.
