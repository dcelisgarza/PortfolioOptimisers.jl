After — one deep generic over the inner estimator

Problem

Five wrappers (681 loc, mostly docstrings) each add a struct, a validator, a keyword constructor and one delegating method. Deletion test: remove a wrapper and its complexity does not reappear in callers — it was a pass-through over the already-shared windowed_preamble.

Solution

A single Windowed{T<:AbstractEstimator}. The preamble runs once; the trailing call dispatches on the inner estimator's type — Julia resolves mean/cov/coskewness automatically.

Wins

  • locality: windowing logic in one place
  • leverage: free for any future estimator
  • delete 5 shallow wrappers
  • validation skew across files impossible
Note: verify the propagation macros (@fprop/@vprop/@wprop, ADR 0002/0010) tag a type-parameter field as cleanly as a concrete one. If they don't, the seam is the generic-vs-tagged-field interaction — worth a spike first.

Problem

Solution

Confirm this doesn't fight the @pprop prior-binding (ADR 0012); the open Kurtosis fold-in item depends on consistent prior propertynames here.

The setup (benchmark extraction, difference, scaling by k, bound wiring) repeats; the cone constraint is the one differing line. A wrong divisor in a rarely-tested norm stays silent.

Problem

~4000 loc across 10 files. The big-M, z-score and cone formulations are re-derived per cell. Update CVaR's z-score and miss RVaR's, and the two diverge with no test catching the co-evolution.

Problem

The Value/Range/Drawdown axis is copied per measure; shared formula fragments live in different files and drift independently.

Solution

Make the two axes explicit: tail-formulation adapter × {Value, Range = two tails, Drawdown = tail-on-drawdowns}. One build_tail! shared across cells.

Wins

  • locality: one tail formula per family
  • Range stops re-deriving the tail twice
  • co-evolution becomes a shared edit
  • leverage: a new VaR family fills a column
Overlaps existing future work — sequence carefully. Two slices are already on the backlog: XatRisk Range = two tail calls via build_tail! (caveat: cones differ per family) and a @risk_measure macro emitting risk_input_kind + supports_precomputed_returns + functor stubs together (ADR 0006/0007). This card is the umbrella; do the macro and build_tail! first, then judge whether the full grid-collapse is still worth it. Speculative precisely because the per-family cone differences may be irreducible.

Top recommendation

Start with Candidate 1 — the drawdown constraint trio.

Smallest blast radius (174 loc, 3 files), highest confidence: the skeleton is provably identical and only the dd[2:T+1] aggregation varies. It deepens a real seam (the aggregation algorithm) with three live adapters, and the test surface drops to one builder. It also rehearses the exact pattern behind Candidate 4 (tracking cones) and Candidate 5 (XatRisk tails) — a low-risk warm-up for the larger constraint-family collapses. Candidate 2 (the Windowed wrapper) is the close runner-up: equally mechanical, larger payoff (681 loc, 5 files), pending only the macro-vs-generic spike.
